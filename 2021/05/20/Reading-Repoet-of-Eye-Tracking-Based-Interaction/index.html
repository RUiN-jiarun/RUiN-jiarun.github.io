Reading Report of Eye Tracking Based Interaction

Original paper: 《Improving User Experience of Eye Tracking-Based Interaction: Introspecting and Adapting Interfaces》

Publication: ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction November 2019

Field:

Why Do I Choose This Paper?

The topic which the paper focuses on is about the application of eye tracking devices. The mix of eye tracking technology and GUI design has become a hot-spot research field of HCI and various of systems and tools have been designed according to this technology.

On the other hand, eye tracking could be a special input device and collect a bunch of data which can not be get from earlier input devices. Studying on those collected data could be a great improvement to many fields such as IoT or WWW.

Furthermore, studying on this paper is a beginning on learning HCI. I hope to find out the research methods and how to raise the awareness of interaction during a system design progress.

Eye Tracking Technology and Application

The paper discussed the possibility to involve eye tracking technology to our daily life. In most situations, eye tracking is considered as a replacement of the regular input device. The paper mentioned the gaze-based pointing and typing methods and the emulation of mouse and keyboard. Both of the previous methods have been evaluated in many ways and put into application.

What’s more, the paper use the analysis method of INSTROPECTION, which requires the rethink of the interface of a gaze-based interaction product. The paper discussed several aspects that eye tracking HCI, such as the improvement of pointing performance, the approaches that users can perceive the interface and inspect options with eyes.

The author focus on enhancing web accessibility with gaze-based interaction and developed a web browser called GazeTheWeb. The product provides accessible web browsing and gaze-controlled web browsing.

GazeTheWeb

To complete this project, the author’s team discuss the technology and properties related to this work.

Eye Gaze Input

Precision and accuracy are two basic properties that gaze interaction need to hold. Many factors should be taken into consideration and need to be checked during evaluation. Research in the field of design and interaction has proposed adaptive designs like enlarged and screen-centered interface elements or visual feedback, to compensate for accuracy issues when it is crucial to determine the attention on a certain
element of an interface that is sized below the accuracy limit of an eye tracking device.

What’s more, eyes play double roles in such a web access progress: as a controller and an observer as well. This is one of the major challenges for gaze-controlled interaction.

Emulation Approach

To replace pointing and typing with eye gazing is extremely difficult to achieved. Thus, it must be considered thoroughly how to joint the emulation of different devices together.

Take searching a web page as an example. There’re at least two different emulation types (mouse and key board), and the user need to change between the two situations. And there’re several action types as well (button. pointing and typing). The whole process is rather complex.

Improvement

To improve the gaze interaction experience, the paper argued for a seamless integration of eye gaze input in the application ecosystem.

  • Reduce the input action overhead by lessening the interpretation gap between the user intention and the interaction elements.
  • Reduce the inspection overhead. Understanding of semantics of interaction elements, we may reduce the time needed for reducing visual search during inspection prior a selection. Both pre-select inspection and post-select inspection need the understanding of semantics.

Interface Introspection

Furthermore, to augmenting the web page interaction, the paper applied the method of interface introspection.

Interface Semantics

As mentioned above, to understand the interface semantics is critically important to reduce the inspection overhead. Considering the dynamics in the modern web pages, the web page interface semantics include retrieval, classification, the tracking of interaction elements as well as the browser control.

Interface Adaptation

An example of adaptation is using gaze-sensitive icons to combine different interaction elements on a single web page.

Similarly, gaze-sensitive icons on a web page are associated with gaze-controlled interface modes for sophisticated gaze-based interaction.

Another method is to adapt the simple web page interaction elements. In some situations, a gaze-sensitive icon is not suitable considering the page space and the ambiguous interpretation. In the following example, the simple web page interaction elements are highlighted and a red point is displayed over their center.

What’s more, the adaptation of the web browser is considered necessarily. Various of interface mode need to be implemented in this project to adjust to different situations.

Evaluation

Comparison with Emulation Approach

The paper compared GazeTheWeb with OptiKey, an indirect gaze control software. The results show that GTW plays a better result in searching, scrolling, back navigation and bookmark editing. It proves that GTW works faster and reduces lots of redundant computation.

Feasibility

The researchers also made some efforts on the proof of the feasibility of GTW. Both lab study and field study were included in this part.

Several TASKS (e-mail, photo, social media and video) were distributed to different kinds of users (gender, age and disability). The result indicated that GTW allowed the participants to successfully perform the real-world tasks on modern web pages with eye tracking as input method.

As for the field study, the daily use of GTW was supervised by the researchers. The data during a system run-time of 186.24 hours was considered. It shows that the frequent usage of GTW during the field study including the visits of various web sites, pages and activities indicated that users were able to adapt to GTW.

However, some problems were found. For example, some interaction websites such as online game website were not suitable for GTW system.

Personal Conclusion and Review

What Do I Gain from the Paper

The project is open-sourced and is published on github. Besides the technics of the project, the researching methods that the paper involved is meaningful for a starter like me.

  • Lab study and field study.
  • Introspection of a certain topic.
  • The proper design of the tasks.

From these aspects, I think that the paper truly DID some effort, but NOT ENOUGH. For example, during the evaluation part, the tasks are not wide enough. Although some daily tasks and easy tasks are set, more complex tasks and tasks required multi input channels and input devices may be tested and considered. Thus I’m going to talk about the drawbacks and some challenges of this project.

Drawbacks and Challenges

It’s a great challenge for users to adapt to a new form of web browsing method. GTW does a good job in adapting to different situations as is shown in the paper. However, I believe that some aspects still need to be considered for GTW and the further development of eye tracking application.

  • The fitting of well-designed website. It is known that many popular websites require additional interaction elements and the design of the web pages are rather complex and more interactive. To fit these website engages more consideration of design.
  • Facing more popular users. Gaze-based interaction is mainly used as a substitution of regular interaction for the disabled. Although GTW might be an important step towards general use of eye tracking in everyday applications, it is still not a good choice for most people, or in another words, NOT USEFUL. From the view of users, a good gaze-based interaction should be a helper rather than a main tool.
  • Not for long-time using. The evaluation results shown in the paper prove that GTW performs good in many aspects, however the evaluation tests were only set in a small range of time (only several tasks, last for no more than 10 mins). It takes more energy to focus on the operation while viewing the web content. For those users who need to use web browser for a long time, GTW may easily lead to tired and distraction.
  • Hardware devices support. Up to now the paper and the project focus on the software development and browser improvement. Another challenge for gaze-based interaction is the support of the devices. Head-wear eye tracker and peripheral eye tracker are totally different in user experience. Thus, different API should be design for different kind of devices.
  • Security. Eye tracking information should be considered as private information. The leak of tracking records could be dangerous for users.

Brainstorm and Ideas

During the reading and studying process, I came up with several ideas. Some of them may be unrealistic or impractical, but some of them may become a good way to improve this technology.

  • More feedbacks. Visual feedback is not enough since the eyes are already act as a signal sender. The sound may act as an additional feedback. Multichannel output and senses may be helpful.

  • Any methods to accelerate typing? Up to now, the typing of gazing-input is still very slow and inconvenient. We hope that some other input devices and methods could be act as an add-on helper. Deep learning based behavior recognitions may be a useful tool.
